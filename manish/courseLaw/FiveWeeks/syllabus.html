15 Oct 02, 2017 Read "Tort Law Overview", "Causation in Tort", "Remedies in Tort", "Liability in Tort", "Strict Liability Causes of Action" and "Intentional Tort Causes of Action" (Chapters 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 and 6, pp. 1 – 43, approx) in Nailing the Bar's Simple Torts Outline (O-2e). (3 hours)

The introductory explanations of tort law are very important, but only tested within the context of other "causes of action". Law professors and modern law often view "strict liability" as a form of negligence. But the core requirement of negligence is a "breach of duty" and strict liability does not require that.
