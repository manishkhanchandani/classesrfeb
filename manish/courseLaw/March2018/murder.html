Murder

Definition

Under CRIMINAL LAW a MURDER is an unlawful HOMICIDE, the killing of one human being by another, with MALICE aforethought.

 

MALICE for murder may be

1) an express INTENT TO KILL,

or implied by 2) intent to commit GREAT BODILY INJURY,

3) a homicide caused by commission of an INHERENTLY DANGEROUS FELONY, the "FELONY MURDER RULE" or

4) DELIBERATE CREATION OF EXTREME RISKS to human life (actus reus) with AWARENESS of the risks and a CONSCIOUS DISREGARD for them (mens rea), the "DEPRAVED HEART" murder.

 

Under COMMON LAW there were NO DEGREES of murder but MODERNLY first degree murder is generally codified as a

1) willful, deliberate and premeditated homicide or one

2) done by enumerated means, e.g. torture, poison and explosives.

3) caused by commission of an enumerated felony.

All other murders are in the second degree.

 

Felony Murder

Murder may be charged under the FELONY-MURDER RULE if a death is caused by the commission of an inherently dangerous felony, but only if the death is caused by acts done during the RES GESTAE of the underlying felony. The RES GESTAE of a crime is the sequence of events from the first substantial step to committing the crime and ending when the defendants leave the scene of the crime and reach a place of a relative safety.

Homicide

Homicide is the killing of one human being by another.

Actual Cause

Actual Cause is used by "but for" test. But for the act of the defendant, the result would have not occured.

Proximate Cause

Proximate cause is the direct and natural result of the act of defendant.

Conspiracy & Murder

If we have two people and one of them doing the crime then read below:

There are two vicarious liability theories:
A. Conspiracy Theory and
B. Accomplice Theory

A. CONSPIRACY THEORY
A person may be charged with vicarious liability for a crime based on CONSPIRACY THEORY if while they are member of a conspiracy, a co-conspirator commits the crime within the scope of the conspiracy. But a conspiracy ends when the criminal goal is ATTAINED or ABANDONED.

Here Dick was a member of a conspiracy to rob the store, but that conspiracy goal was abandoned when they found the store closed. Since the conspiracy had ended, Tom did not kill the Victor the next day "within the scope" of the conspiracy.

Therefore Dick cannot be charged on this basis.

B. ACCOMPLICE THEORY
A person who commits or helps commit a crime is liable for all subsequent crimes by co-felons that are a direct and natural result of the prior crimes.

Here Dick helped Tom to rob the store the prior day, so he would be liable for any crimes by Tom that are a direct and natural result of the attempted robbery. But the murder of Victor was not a foreseeable result of the prior robbery attempt.

Therefore Dick cannot be charged with Victor's death based on accomplice liability.

Since Dick had no direct liability or vicarious liability he cannot be charged with Murder.

VOLUNTARY MANSLAUGHTER
Under criminal law VOLUNTARY MANSLAUGHTER is an unlawful, intentional homicide without malice aforethought because of adequate provocation sufficient to raise a reasonable person to a fit of rage which could and actually did cause the homicide.

Generally "mere words alone" are not considered at law to be adequate provocation.

Elements:

  • defendant committed murder
  • adequate provocation
  • defendant lost self-control (subjective test)
  • a reasonable person would lose self-control (objective test)
  • no cool off period

Analysis
Here there was a lack of adequate provocation because "hard looks", being "dissed", challenged to a "spelling bee" and criticisms of attire" are "mere words alone" that would not be sufficient to raise a reasonable person to a fit of murderous rage. The subjective factor that Killer had a "short fuse" would not lower the standard of conduct.

Therefore, Killer would not succeed in reducing the murder charge to voluntary manslaughter.

Counter analysis

Here there was a adequate provocation because "sleeping of wife with friend" would be sufficient to raise a reasonable person to a fit of murderous rage.

Therefore, Killer would succeed in reducing the murder charge to voluntary manslaughter.

INVOLUNTARY MANSLAUGHTER

Under criminal law, Involuntary manslaughter is an unintentional homicide resulting from GROSS NEGLIGENCE, RECKLESSNESS or the commission of a MALUM IN SE crime insufficient to support the felony murder rule.

GROSS (CRIMINAL) NEGLIGENCE is the deliberate breach of a pre-existing duty to protect others from extreme risks, and RECKLESSNESS is the deliberate creation of extreme risks to others.
