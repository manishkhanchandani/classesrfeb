EQUISETUM HYEMALE (Equis.)

 

 

URINARY TRACT & BLADDER AFFECTIONS

 

 

MENTAL

 

Tired, restless and prostrated with aversion to mental work.

 

Prostration of mind, mental exhaustion, brain- fatigue.

Irritable, restless and yet prostrated. Don't like to be touched.

 

Disposition to frown.

 

 

PHYSICAL

 

A remedy for cystitis, enuresis and dysuria.

Very frequent urination but very little urine is voided (Nux-v.). Desire to urinate increases as the quantity of urine diminishes.

Pain in urethra after urination.

 

Retention of urine in old and tired people, after delivery. Fullness sensation in the bladder which prevents urination and it is relieved if they manage to urinate.

Paralysis of bladder in old people.

 

Involuntary urination at night (Rhus-t., Tub., Puls., Kreos., Bell.), without a tangible cause, especially in restless children.

 

Mucous sediment in urine.

 

EQUISETUM HYEMALE

(Equis.)

(Scouring Rush)

Principal action is on the bladder. A remedy for enuresis and dysuria.

Urinary
Severe, dull pain and feeling of fullness in the bladder, not relieved by urinating, Frequent urging with severe pain AT THE CLOSE OF URINATION. Urine flows only drop by drop. Sharp, BURNING, cutting pains in the urethra while urinating. Incontinence in children, with dreams or nightmares when passing urine. Incontinence in old women, also with involuntary stools. Retention and dysuria during pregnancy and after delivery. Much mucus in urine. Albuminuria. Involuntary urination (enuresis).
Deep pain in the region of right kidney, extending to the lower abdomen, with an urgent desire to micturate. Right lumbar region painful.

Modalities
AGG.: right side; movement, pressure, touch, sitting down;
AMEL.: in the afternoon from lying down.

Relationship
Compare: HYDRANG., FERR-P., APIS, CANTH., LINA., CHIM. EQUIS. contains silica in an appreciable quantity.

Dose
Tincture, to sixth potency. A decoction, teaspoonful doses, or the tincture in hot water is found useful to allay irritability of urinary tract, calculus, dysuria, etc., also for pleuritic effusion and dropsy.
