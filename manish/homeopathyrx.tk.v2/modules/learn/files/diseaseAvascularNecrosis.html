Avascular necrosis

What is Avascular necrosis?
The death of bone tissue due to a lack of blood supply.

Common Facts:
Avascular necrosis is associated with long-term steroid use and drinking too much alcohol. It's most common in people between the ages of 30 and 60 and often affects the hip.

Early stages may be symptom-free. Eventually, the affected joint may hurt when weight is put on it or when lying down.

Treatment includes physical therapy, surgery, and medications

Rare

Fewer than 200,000 US cases per year

Symptoms

Requires a medical diagnosis
Early stages may be symptom-free. Eventually, the affected joint may hurt when weight is put on it or when lying down.

People may experience:
Pain areas: in the hip, buttocks, groin, or knee

Also common: collapse, limping, or thigh pain

Ages Affected: 41 to 60 years

  • Can't be cured, but treatment may help
  • Requires a medical diagnosis
  • Lab tests or imaging always required
  • Chronic: can last for years or be lifelong

Self Care:
Avoid smoking

Limit alcohol intake
