VI. NECESSITY Necessity is the privilege to make a reasonable use of the property of others to prevent immediate harm or damage to persons or property. Public Necessity: 2 Interference with one’s real or personal property is necessary to prevent a disaster to the community or to a large number of people Example: Without permission, you use a stranger’s car to drive a member of your family to the emergency room of a hospital. Example 2: Without permission, you burn the crops of a neighbor in order to stop the spread of a fire that is headed toward the town on the other side of the neighbor’s field containing the crops.
